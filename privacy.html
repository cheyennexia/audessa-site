Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 10, 2026

Audessa is a Chrome extension that helps users listen to webpage articles using browser-provided text-to-speech voices. A mobile article queue is planned for a future release.

Information Audessa Processes

When you activate Audessa for Chrome on a webpage, the extension processes article text in your browser to extract readable content and play it aloud.

Audessa stores extension preferences such as selected voice, playback speed, word highlighting, high contrast, dyslexia-friendly font, and text size using Chrome extension storage.

Audessa collects your email address only if you voluntarily submit it through a waitlist or notification form.

How Information Is Used

Article text is used to provide the text-to-speech reading feature. The Chrome reader processes article text in your browser.

Saved preferences are used to remember your settings across browsing sessions.

Submitted email addresses are used only to notify users about Audessa updates, premium voice availability, mobile app availability, or related product news.

Data Sharing

Audessa does not sell user data.

Email waitlist submissions are processed through Audessa-controlled Supabase infrastructure. Limited request metadata may be processed to prevent abuse and protect the waitlist endpoint.

Data Storage

Extension preferences are stored using Chrome extension storage.

Waitlist email addresses are stored in Audessa-controlled Supabase infrastructure.

User Choices

You can remove saved extension preferences by uninstalling the extension or clearing extension data in Chrome.

You can request removal from the waitlist by contacting Audessa support.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact hello@audessa.app.
